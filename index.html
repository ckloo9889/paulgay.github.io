About me

I graduated in computer sciences from INSA Rouen (France) in 2011 and obtained at the same time a M. of Sciences from university of Rouen.
Then, I started a phd under the supervision of Sylvain Meigner (LIUM, France) and Jean Marc Odobez (IDIAP Research Institute, Switzerland). My research there focused on investigating algorithms for unsupervised Audio-visual person identification in broadcast data. The major part of this work consisted in improving speaker diarization and face clustering systems. I used probabilistic graphical models to integrate the different modalities in a global framework. This work took place in the context of the REPERE evaluation campaign.
During the school year 2014-2015, I was a teaching assistant (ATER) at Avignon university and member of the LIA lab.
I am currently a post-doctoral fellow at PAVIS/IIT (Genova Italia) where I work on probabistic matrix factorization, with an application on structure from motion and motion segmentation.
